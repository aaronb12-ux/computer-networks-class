Principles of Reliable Data Transfer (Part 2)

we now want to extend our reliable data transfer protcol to cover the case that not only can the channel currupt messages
but the channel can also lose messages. This is going to apply to both the data flowing from sender to receiver as well as acknowledgements that are flowing from the receiver back
to the sender.
The rdt3.0 protcol handles the case of lost packets

rdt3.0: channels with errors and loss
  New Channel Assumption: underlying channel can also lose packets (data, ACKs)
  Approach: sender waits 'reasonable' amount of time for ACK
  if pkt (or ACK) just delayed (not lost):
    retransmission will be duplicate, but seq #s already handles this!
    receiver must specify seq # of packet being ACKed
