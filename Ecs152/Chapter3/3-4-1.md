Principles of Reliable Data Transfer (part 1)

Challenge: How do two distributed entities reliably communicate over a channel which itself is unreliable

Sending process wants to send data to the receiving process through a reliable channel (uni directional) 

The 'reliable channel' is an abstraction: here is the implementation. It is in the form of a transport layer protocol
There are two sides: a sender-side of reliable data transfer protocol and a receiver-side. Both sender and receiver sides are communicating with eachother over an unreliable channel

<img width="338" height="255" alt="Screenshot 2026-04-16 at 1 18 43 PM" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c44baaf7-f3ea-4a17-8288-2090b330ef2b" />

The complexity of reliable data transfer protocol will depend (strongly) on characteristics of unreliable channel (lose, corrupt, reorder data?) 

Sender, receiver do not know the "state" of each other, e.g. was a message received?
  unless communicated via a message


Reliable Data Transfer Protocol (rdt): interfaces

On the sending side, data is being passed down from an application layer process to the transport layer. The transport layer then adds a header together with the data to create a
transport layer segment and sends that over the unreliable channnel to the receiver. 

On the receiving side when a segment does pop out, it will have a header and also a data component to the segment. The receiver side then dlivers data up to the receiving process at the application layer

Reliable Data Transfer: Getting Started
  we will:
    incramentally develop sender, receiver sides of reliable data transfer protcol (rdt)
    consider only unidirectiondal data transfer
      but control into will flow on both directions!
    use finite state machines (FSM) to specify sender, receiver

rdt1.0: reliable transfer over a reliable channel
  underlying channel perfectly reliable
    no bit errors
    no loss of packets
  separate FSMs for sender, receiver:
    sender sends data into underlying channel
    receiver reads data from underlying channel


rdt2.0: channel with bit errors
  underlying channel may flip bits in packet
    checksum to detect erros
    the question: how to recover from errors
      acklowledgements (ACKs): receiver explicitly tells sender that pkt received OK
      negative acklowledgements (NAKs): receiver explicitly tells sender that pkt had errors
      sender retransmits pkt on receipt of NAK

      step and wait: sender sends on packet, then waits for receier response
